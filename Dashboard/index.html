Urban Heat Island (UHI) Effect

Urban districts like Dong Da, Cau Giay, and Thanh Xuan show surface temperatures (LST) exceeding 39°C. This is directly caused by the replacement of natural greenery and water bodies with concrete and asphalt, which absorb and re-emit solar radiation.

Drainage Degradation & Flooding

Hanoi suffers from severe flash floods in the rainy season. Areas with concretization rates exceeding 70% (e.g. Hoang Mai, Dong Da, Cau Giay) show the highest frequency of flooding points. Soil impermeability prevents stormwater infiltration, overloading the artificial drainage pipes.

Air Quality and Urbanization Correlation

Annual average AQI correlates strongly with population density and traffic nodes. Central urban districts exceed an AQI of 135, representing "Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups", whereas forested districts like Ba Vi register below 70 ("Moderate" to "Good").

Solid Waste Footprint

Hanoi generates over 8,000 tons of household solid waste daily. The dense cores of Dong Da, Hoang Mai, and Ha Dong each produce 500-650 tons daily, placing immense logistical pressures on local landfill and incinerator facilities.
